Mohiniyattam - Classical Dance of Kerala

Mohiniyattam is a classical dance form from the southern Indian state of Kerala. It is known for its graceful and fluid movements, feminine postures, and storytelling through dance.

Characteristics of Mohiniyattam:

  • Costume and Makeup: Mohiniyattam dancers wear off-white or cream-colored sarees with gold borders and jewelry. The makeup is minimal, with a focus on enhancing facial expressions and features.
  • Music and Instruments: The dance is accompanied by traditional Carnatic music. Instruments like the mridangam, violin, flute, and veena are often used to create a melodic and rhythmic backdrop for the performance.
  • Theme and Repertoire: Mohiniyattam often portrays themes from mythology and Hindu epics. The dance form is known for its storytelling abilities, and the dancer uses hand gestures and expressions to convey the narrative.

History:

  • Origin: Mohiniyattam is rooted in Kerala's rich cultural heritage and is believed to have originated in the 16th century. It evolved from the Devadasi tradition, where temple dancers performed as offerings to deities.
  • Name: The name "Mohiniyattam" is derived from the words "Mohini" (the enchantress) and "attam" (dance), referring to the dance of the enchantress. The dance form often depicts feminine grace and beauty.
  • Influence: Mohiniyattam has been influenced by various forms of art and culture, including the classical Sanskrit text Natya Shastra. It is characterized by its use of hand gestures (mudras), facial expressions, and elegant movements.

Famous Mohiniyattam Dancers:

  • Kalamandalam Kalyanikutty Amma: Kalamandalam Kalyanikutty Amma is considered one of the pioneers of the revival of Mohiniyattam. She received several awards for her contributions to the art form.
  • Dr. Kanak Rele: Dr. Kanak Rele is a renowned Mohiniyattam exponent and choreographer. She has played a significant role in popularizing the dance form and has received numerous accolades for her work.
  • Padma Menon: Padma Menon is a contemporary Mohiniyattam dancer who has gained recognition for her innovative choreography and efforts to make the dance form more accessible to wider audiences.
