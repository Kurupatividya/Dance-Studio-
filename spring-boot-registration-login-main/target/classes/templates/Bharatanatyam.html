Bharatanatyam Dance - Classical Art of India

Bharatanatyam is one of the major classical dance forms of India, known for its intricate movements, expressive storytelling, and rich cultural heritage.

Characteristics of Bharatanatyam Dance:

  • Mudras and Expressions: Bharatanatyam is known for its use of "mudras" (hand gestures) and facial expressions to convey stories, emotions, and themes.
  • Costume and Makeup: Dancers wear traditional Indian costumes with vibrant colors, adorned with jewelry. Makeup is used to enhance facial expressions and features.
  • Rhythmic Footwork: The dance incorporates intricate and rhythmic footwork, and dancers often wear ankle bells (ghungroo) to accentuate the foot movements.

History:

  • Origin: Bharatanatyam has its roots in the temples of South India and is believed to have originated over 2,000 years ago. It was traditionally performed as a form of worship and storytelling.
  • Evolution: Over the centuries, Bharatanatyam underwent transformations and adaptations. It faced challenges but experienced a revival in the 20th century through the efforts of dedicated artists and gurus.
  • Styles: Bharatanatyam is practiced in various styles, with the Pandanallur, Kalakshetra, and Vazhuvoor styles being some of the prominent ones.

Famous Bharatanatyam Dancers:

  • Rukmini Devi Arundale: She played a pivotal role in the revival of Bharatanatyam in the 20th century and is known for her efforts in promoting the dance form on a global scale.
  • Mallika Sarabhai: Mallika Sarabhai is a renowned Bharatanatyam dancer who is also known for her social activism and contemporary interpretations of the dance.
  • Vyjayanthimala Bali: Vyjayanthimala is a famous actress and Bharatanatyam dancer. She was one of the first Indian actresses to be internationally recognized.
  • Padma Subrahmanyam: Padma Subrahmanyam is a prominent Bharatanatyam exponent known for her research and innovations in the dance form.
