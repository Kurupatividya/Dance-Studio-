Odissi Dance - Classical Art of Odisha

Odissi is a classical dance form from the state of Odisha in eastern India. It has a rich history and is known for its expressive and intricate movements.

Characteristics of Odissi Dance:

  • Mudras and Expressions: Odissi is known for its use of "mudras" (hand gestures) and "bhavas" (expressions) to convey stories and emotions. Dancers use their hands, face, and body to portray a wide range of characters and narratives.
  • Costume and Jewelry: Odissi dancers wear traditional Odissi attire, which includes colorful silk sarees, intricate jewelry, and distinctive makeup. The costume and makeup are designed to enhance the beauty and expressiveness of the dancer.
  • Music and Instruments: Odissi is accompanied by traditional Odissi music, which includes classical Odissi ragas and talas. Instruments like the mardala (a percussion instrument), flute, and violin are commonly used in Odissi performances.

History:

  • Origin: Odissi has its roots in the ancient temples of Odisha, where it was performed as a form of worship and storytelling. It is believed to have originated over 2,000 years ago and is closely associated with the temple traditions of the region.
  • Evolution: Over the centuries, Odissi underwent various changes and transformations. It faced a decline during the British colonial period but experienced a revival in the 20th century through the efforts of dedicated artists and gurus.

Famous Odissi Dancers:

  • Guru Kelucharan Mohapatra: Guru Kelucharan Mohapatra is considered one of the most prominent Odissi dancers and gurus. He played a pivotal role in the revival of Odissi and trained numerous students.
  • Sanjukta Panigrahi: Sanjukta Panigrahi was a celebrated Odissi dancer and one of the leading exponents of the dance form. She made significant contributions to popularizing Odissi both in India and internationally.
  • Sonal Mansingh: Sonal Mansingh is a renowned dancer who has excelled in both Odissi and Bharatanatyam. She has received numerous awards and honors for her contributions to the field of dance.
