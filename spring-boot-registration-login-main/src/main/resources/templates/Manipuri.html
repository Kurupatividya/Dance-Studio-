Manipuri Dance - Classical Art of Manipur

Manipuri is a classical dance form originating from the northeastern Indian state of Manipur. It is known for its graceful and lyrical movements, its devotion to Lord Krishna, and its distinct cultural and religious significance.

Characteristics of Manipuri Dance:

  • Rasas: Manipuri dance is characterized by the expression of "Rasas" or emotional states. Dancers convey different emotions, such as love, devotion, and joy, through their expressions and movements.
  • Costumes: Dancers wear traditional Manipuri costumes, including rich and colorful skirts, ornaments, and headpieces. The male dancers often wear dhotis and turbans.
  • Instruments: Manipuri dance is accompanied by traditional Manipuri music, which includes the use of instruments like the pung (a barrel-shaped drum), cymbals, and stringed instruments.

History:

  • Origin: Manipuri dance has its roots in the ancient traditions of Manipur and is closely tied to Vaishnavism, a form of Hinduism that reveres Lord Krishna. It has a long history dating back to the 2nd century CE.
  • Religious Significance: Manipuri dance is deeply intertwined with the religious and cultural practices of the region. It is often performed in the temples of Manipur and is an essential part of religious ceremonies and festivals.

Famous Manipuri Dancers:

  • Ratna Kumar: Ratna Kumar is a renowned Manipuri dancer who has contributed significantly to the promotion and preservation of Manipuri dance. She has trained numerous students and is recognized for her expertise in the art form.
  • Darshana Jhaveri: Darshana Jhaveri is a well-known Manipuri dancer who has played a vital role in popularizing the dance form not only in India but also internationally. She has received several awards for her contributions to the art of Manipuri dance.
