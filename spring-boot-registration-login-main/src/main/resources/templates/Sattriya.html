Sattriya Dance - Classical Art of Assam

Sattriya is a classical Indian dance form that originated in the state of Assam. It is closely associated with the Vaishnavite monasteries or "sattras" of Assam.

Characteristics of Sattriya Dance:

  • Costume and Attire: Sattriya dancers typically wear traditional Assamese attire, which includes intricate and colorful costumes and jewelry. The dancers often use masks, especially in the dance dramas.
  • Dance Movements: Sattriya is characterized by graceful and gentle movements, which are performed with precision and expressiveness. It includes a wide range of hand gestures (mudras) and footwork.
  • Music and Instruments: The music for Sattriya dance is based on traditional Assamese and devotional compositions. Instruments like the khol (a barrel-shaped drum), cymbals, and flute are commonly used in Sattriya performances.

History:

  • Origin: Sattriya dance is believed to have originated in the 15th century in the Vaishnavite monasteries of Assam, particularly in the sattras established by the saint and scholar Srimanta Sankardev. It was initially performed as a form of worship and storytelling within the religious context.
  • Evolution: Over the centuries, Sattriya evolved into a classical dance form. It incorporates elements of dance, drama, and music and is known for its devotional and spiritual themes. Srimanta Sankardev and his disciple Madhavdev made significant contributions to the development of Sattriya.

Famous Sattriya Dancers:

  • Guru Bipin Singh: Guru Bipin Singh was a highly respected Sattriya and Manipuri dancer and choreographer. He played a pivotal role in bringing Sattriya to the mainstream and promoting its recognition as a classical dance form.
  • Jatin Goswami: Jatin Goswami is a prominent Sattriya dancer known for his contributions to the art form. He has received acclaim for his performances and efforts to preserve and promote Sattriya dance.
